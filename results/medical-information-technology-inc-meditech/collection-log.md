## Product Context

MEDITECH is one of the largest EHR vendors in the United States, particularly dominant in community hospitals and mid-size health systems. The company offers a comprehensive healthcare information system (HCIS) that spans clinical, financial, and administrative functions.

**MEDITECH Expanse** (the current flagship product) includes:
- **Clinical**: Full EHR with physician documentation, nursing, order entry, clinical decision support
- **Revenue Cycle**: Patient accounting, billing, claims management, payment posting, collections
- **Ambulatory/Practice Management**: Scheduling, ambulatory workflows
- **Patient Connect**: Patient portal with messaging, appointment scheduling, health record access
- **Pharmacy**: Inpatient and ambulatory pharmacy
- **Emergency Department**: ED management
- **Oncology**: Oncology-specific workflows
- **Pathology/Laboratory**: Lab results, pathology, microbiology
- **Imaging/Radiology**: PACS integration, radiology workflow
- **Population Health**: Health analytics and population management
- **Surgical Services**: OR scheduling, surgical documentation
- **Home Care and Hospice**, **Mental Health**, **Post-Acute** care settings

The product also spans multiple historical platform generations:
- **MEDITECH Expanse 2.2/2.1** (current)
- **MEDITECH 6.15/6.08** (prior generation)
- **MEDITECH Client/Server (C/S) 5.67**
- **MEDITECH MAGIC 5.67** (legacy)
- **MEDITECH MAGIC HCA** (HCA Healthcare variant)

The certified modules collectively cover the full HCIS — Core HCIS, ED Management, Ambulatory, Oncology, and the MyHealth Portal.

## Obstacles & Notes

1. **No anti-bot protection**: All pages and PDFs accessible via simple curl with standard User-Agent.
2. **Customer-only operational guides**: The "Regulatory EHI Export Functionality Guides" at `customer.meditech.com` require SAML authentication. However, the publicly available documentation (the 3 HTML pages + 3 PDFs) appears to contain the data content specifications. The customer-only guides are likely operational/procedural (how to run the export), not data specification documents.
3. **Two-configuration model**: MEDITECH splits documentation into two configurations based on the underlying infrastructure (HIM/SCN vs. MRI/DR). Configuration 2 (older platforms) provides more granular field-level documentation via the CSV PDFs, while Configuration 1 (newer platforms) describes the export at a section/folder level without field-level CSV detail.
4. **No field-level documentation for Expanse**: The newest and most widely deployed platform (Expanse) falls under Configuration 1, which documents folder structures and file types but does NOT provide a CSV table/column specification. The detailed PDFs are only available for the older platforms (C/S, MAGIC, MPM 6.08). This is a significant gap in documentation.
5. **Export uses text/PDF reports rather than structured data for some sections**: Financial Reports are exported as FinancialEHI.txt (a "Patient Accounting report"), Authorization data as PDFs, Immunization History as PDF. This suggests some data is exported as rendered reports rather than structured/machine-readable tabular data.
